Technical Deep Dive

The Strategy Subsystem

Inside the digital brain driving real-time intelligence in solar endurance racing.

The Strategy Subsystem is the command centre of our vehicle—the system that decides, every second, how fast to go, how efficiently to use power, and how best to harness the sun.

What began as a physics-based simulator has evolved into an ML-driven framework that fuses aerodynamics, energy flow, and solar dynamics to calculate the optimal speed for every kilometre of a race. This isn't just software; it's cognition in motion!

From Equations to Intelligence

Built entirely in Python, the first version of our simulator used the Newton-Raphson method to compute race speeds for a 150 km endurance run while maintaining 60% battery charge at the finish line. It modelled aerodynamic drag, rolling resistance, terrain effects, and real-world motor and battery behaviour, forming a digital twin of our vehicle that laid the foundation for machine learning to take over.

When the Machine Started Learning

To move beyond pure computation, we generated 50,000+ synthetic race simulations and trained machine learning models to predict optimal speeds under varying gradients, irradiance, and wind conditions.

The Random Forest Regressor achieved an exceptional $R^{2}=0.999$, performing in milliseconds what physics solvers took minutes to compute, transforming strategy from calculation to real-time cognition.

Embracing Uncertainty

Real races are unpredictable, so our subsystem evolved into an Uncertainty-Aware AI Framework:

Data Augmentation & Hyperparameter Optimisation
Monte Carlo Markov Chains (MCMC)
Stochastic Model Predictive Control (MPC)

Current Achievements

  • ✓ Generated custom datasets using advanced simulators.
  • ✓ Benchmarked Linear Regression, Random Forest, and MCMC models.
  • ✓ Conducted sensitivity analyses and uncertainty modelling.

These steps built a solid foundation for data-driven decision-making in solar endurance racing.

What's Next

  • → Real-world telemetry datasets capturing driver and power dynamics.
  • → Explainable AI (XAI) for transparent model interpretation.
  • → Advanced forecasting models (ARIMA, SVM, LSTM).
  • → Research on Bayesian control and fair-by-design charge scheduling.

The Strategy Subsystem represents more than engineering — it's the embodiment of smart racing.

By combining the precision of physics with the adaptability of machine learning, it transforms uncertainty into confidence and data into speed.
